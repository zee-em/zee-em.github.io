Visual Design for Pico-8 workshops

Promotional materials in support of community focused coding workshops.

My collaborator Don Miller and I ran community workshops in Amsterdam, the Netherlands, and Gainesville, Florida. I created print and digital assets to publicize the events, including a basic event webpage.

Working at Mozilla

During my seven years at the tech nonprofit Mozilla, I developed leadership curriculum and taught workshops. I wrote articles, blogged, co-produced videos, and even made a podcast pilot. Much of my work revolved around community events like Mozilla Festival and our global coding sprint.

Read my recap of our 2018 Global Sprint You can learn more about MozFest in The Making of MozFest. I blogged about two 2018 Festival contributors in Navigating AI’s Moral Labyrinth at MozFest and At MozFest and Beyond: Defending the Internet from Machitrolls. The image at right is from the MozFest 10th anniversary book, to which I also contributed.

My colleague Chad Sanasing and experimented with a pilot MozCast podcast. I also co-produced two videos with my colleague Abby Cabunoc Mayes. We developed concept and content, working closely with an external videographer and animator.

Plant Signage

Little print signs introducing some plants, created for a garden/nursery in Gainesville, Florida.

I created the custom font to match the client’s aesthetic, as the gardens are part of a complex that features huge, very colorful geometric tile mosaics and art made from recycled/found materials.
